# Market and ICP Map (2026-03-15)

## Goal

Identify where PhotonTrust can win first and who is most likely to buy.

## Primary Category

Trusted decision infrastructure for photonic and quantum systems.

## Core Buyer Problem

Organizations can often generate technical outputs, but they struggle to turn
those outputs into defensible, auditable, cross-functional decisions.

PhotonTrust should sell into that decision bottleneck.

## Priority ICPs

### ICP 1: quantum networking and secure communications teams

Examples:

- telecom advanced engineering teams
- national secure communications programs
- defense-adjacent contractors
- quantum networking startups

Pain points:

- comparing architectures under realistic assumptions
- documenting tradeoffs for internal and external stakeholders
- producing trustable evidence for deployment-oriented decisions

Why PhotonTrust fits:

- QKD and system modeling already exist in the product
- compare/certify/evidence export is directly relevant

### ICP 2: PIC and photonic hardware teams moving toward foundry readiness

Examples:

- photonic startups
- silicon photonics design teams
- systems companies with integrated PIC programs

Pain points:

- moving from design intent to manufacturable evidence
- connecting design, layout, GDS, and verification outputs
- explaining foundry-readiness status internally and externally

Why PhotonTrust fits:

- graph to layout to GDS path already exists
- PDK/component story is growing
- evidence and decision framing is differentiated

### ICP 3: orbit-aware and satellite-linked photonic programs

Examples:

- space communications teams
- secure satellite link programs
- mission planning and system integration teams

Pain points:

- comparing dynamic operational scenarios
- validating assumptions across providers and environments
- packaging mission decisions into reviewable artifacts

Why PhotonTrust fits:

- orbit and satellite-chain support already exist
- provider parity and validation lanes are strong technical assets

## Secondary ICPs

### procurement / assurance / evaluation teams

These may not be the first direct users, but they may become budget holders once
PhotonTrust proves itself with technical teams.

## Budget Owner Hypothesis

Likely first budget owners:

- engineering leadership
- advanced R&D program leads
- systems integration leads
- product/platform heads in photonics or secure infrastructure programs

Likely later budget owners:

- procurement / assurance / compliance leadership
- platform and deployment teams

## Wedge Prioritization

### First wedge to sell

Compare + certify + export evidence for high-stakes engineering decisions.

### Second wedge to expand into

PIC / foundry-readiness workflow continuity.

### Third wedge to expand into

orbit-aware and satellite-linked scenario assurance.
