# 20M Funding Thesis (2026-03-15)

## Objective

Frame PhotonTrust as a company that can credibly justify a `20M EUR` raise as a
scale-up round for a strategic deep-tech platform, not as a speculative research
grant substitute.

## One-Sentence Thesis

PhotonTrust is building the trust, verification, and decision infrastructure for
photonic and quantum systems, turning simulation, layout, compare, and evidence
generation into a deployable platform that organizations can use to choose,
justify, certify, and operationalize high-stakes engineering decisions.

## Why This Matters Now

The market is moving from isolated research results to integrated photonic and
quantum systems that must survive procurement, manufacturing, deployment, and
audit scrutiny. The bottleneck is no longer only computing an answer; it is
producing a defensible decision.

PhotonTrust sits in that bottleneck.

## Problem Statement

Teams building photonic and quantum systems struggle to:

1. compare architecture options under realistic assumptions,
2. connect simulation outputs to manufacturing and deployment constraints,
3. preserve provenance and evidence trails across teams,
4. justify decisions to internal leadership, customers, foundries, and public
   programs,
5. repeat this process quickly enough for real engineering and procurement cycles.

Today this is usually handled with fragmented notebooks, one-off scripts,
spreadsheets, slide decks, and manually assembled reports.

## Product Claim

PhotonTrust is not only a simulator. It is the layer that connects:

- modeling,
- engineering workflows,
- compare and signoff,
- manufacturing artifacts,
- and publishable evidence.

That combination is the core strategic asset.

## Category Definition

Recommended category framing:

**Trusted decision infrastructure for photonic and quantum systems**

Alternative secondary framings:

- verification and certification layer for quantum networking and photonic design
- control plane for photonic and quantum engineering decisions

## Initial Wedge

Recommended first wedge:

**Compare -> certify -> export evidence for photonic/quantum system decisions**

Why this wedge is strong:

1. it is hard to do well with ad hoc tools,
2. it connects directly to budget owners and signoff processes,
3. it expands naturally into simulation, PDK/foundry readiness, and deployment
   planning,
4. it creates a moat around trust and workflow continuity rather than raw
   numerical output alone.

- architecture evaluation

## Moat Hypothesis

PhotonTrust's moat is not just numerical modeling. It is the integrated stack of:

1. simulation and execution,
2. graph and API orchestration,
3. compare and signoff workflow,
4. evidence and provenance generation,
5. manufacturability and PDK-aware paths,
6. benchmark and parity validation.

That is much harder to replace with notebooks or internal scripts than any one of
the layers in isolation.

## Why 20M Could Be Rational

A `20M EUR` ask becomes rational if PhotonTrust is framed as the platform that
bridges research-grade modeling and deployment-grade decision infrastructure
across several adjacent high-value markets:

- secure communications and QKD infrastructure,
- photonic manufacturing and foundry-readiness,
- orbit-aware and satellite-linked photonic systems,
- assurance, procurement, and certification workflows.

The raise then funds scale, integrations, enterprise readiness, and go-to-market
expansion, not basic product discovery.
